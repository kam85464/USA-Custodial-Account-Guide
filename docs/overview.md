# Overview: U.S. Custodial Accounts (UGMA/UTMA)

A custodial account is a financial account where:
- An adult (custodian) manages the assets  
- A minor (beneficiary) legally owns the assets  
- Control transfers at age 18–25 depending on state  

## Key Benefits
- Flexible usage (not restricted to education)  
- Works even if you relocate internationally
- Child can access funds from abroad  
- Simple setup, no legal trust required  
- Broad investment options (stocks, ETFs, bonds)  

## Why It Works for Immigrant Parents
- Account remains active after relocation  
- U.S. markets offer strong long-term growth  
- Child can take ownership online from abroad
