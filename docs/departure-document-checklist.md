# Departure Document Checklist

Before you close your U.S. residence, download and digitally secure the following documents. These are vital for maintaining your custodial account, filing future taxes, and proving residency if challenged by a brokerage.

## 1. Financial & Brokerage Records
- [ ] **Consolidated 1099s:** Download for the last 3 years (for the custodial account and your personal accounts).
- [ ] **Monthly Statements:** Last 12 months of statements for the UGMA/UTMA account.
- [ ] **Account Opening Applications:** A PDF copy of the original application (shows the state of jurisdiction and SSN links).
- [ ] **Cost Basis Reports:** If not included in statements, download a report showing the purchase price of all securities.

## 2. Banking Records
- [ ] **Bank Statements:** Last 24 months of statements for the bank account linked to your brokerage.
- [ ] **1099-INT/1099-OID:** Tax forms for interest earned.
- [ ] **Direct Deposit Records:** Proof of the source of funds (W-2 history).
- [ ] **Voided Check:** A digital scan of a voided check for the account (useful for linking new accounts later).

## 3. Tax & Income Records
- [ ] **Federal & State Returns:** Complete copies of Form 1040 and state returns for the last 5 years.
- [ ] **W-2s and 1095-C:** Your wage and health insurance coverage statements.
- [ ] **FBAR/FATCA Records:** If you previously filed these, keep copies.
- [ ] **Form 1040-C (Sailing Permit):** The "Departing Alien Income Tax Return." Technically required to be filed before leaving the U.S. to certify tax compliance.
- [ ] **Dual-Status Filing Records:** Documentation for both the resident (1040) and non-resident (1040-NR) portions of your departure year.
- [ ] **Form 8840 (Closer Connection):** If applicable, to prove you have a closer connection to your new country than the U.S. for tax purposes.
- [ ] **Form 8854 (Expatriation Statement):** If you are a long-term resident (Green Card holder for 8 of the last 15 years) or a U.S. citizen.

## 4. Identity & Legal Documents
- [ ] **Social Security Cards:** High-resolution scans of the physical cards (Custodian and Child).
- [ ] **Immigration History:** Copies of all I-797 Approval Notices, I-94 history, and Visa stamps.
- [ ] **Birth Certificate:** Digital copy of the child’s birth certificate (to prove relationship for the custodial account).

## 5. Proof of Residency (The "Paper Trail")
- [ ] **Final Utility Bills:** Last 3 months of electric, water, or internet bills showing your name and the U.S. address.
- [ ] **Lease or Closing Disclosure:** Proof of when you officially moved out.

## ⚠️ Secure Storage Recommendation
These documents contain **Highly Sensitive PII** (Personally Identifiable Information). 
*   **Do not** store these in unencrypted cloud folders (like standard Google Drive/Dropbox) without additional protection.
*   **Use** an encrypted vault (e.g., Bitwarden, 1Password, or an encrypted 7-Zip/DMG file).
*   **Offline Backup:** Keep a copy on a physical, encrypted USB drive stored in a secure location in India.

## 6. Health & Education (For Child's Transition)
- [ ] **Immunization Records:** Crucial for admission into Indian international or private schools.
- [ ] **School Transcripts:** For credit transfers.
