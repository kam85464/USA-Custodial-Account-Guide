# International Strategy: Maintaining a U.S. Custodial Account from Abroad

## Before Leaving the U.S.
- Open custodial account (UGMA)  
- Maintain U.S. bank account  
- Set up trusted U.S. mailing address  
- Store all documents securely  
- **Designate Successor Custodian:** Use the brokerage's form to name a backup manager (e.g., a spouse) to prevent account lockouts if you are incapacitated.
- Enable online access + 2FA  

## After Moving Abroad
- **The Address Challenge:** 
  - Banks and brokerages require a physical U.S. address under "Know Your Customer" (KYC) rules.
  - **Strategy:** Many use a trusted friend or family member's address. 
  - **Warning:** Avoid using commercial "Virtual Mailboxes" for the *physical* address, as many financial institutions flag these and may freeze the account. Use them only for *mailing* if necessary.
- Keep U.S. bank account active  
- Continue investing (ETFs allowed for existing clients)  
- **Update Tax Status:** Submit Form **W-8BEN** to the brokerage to notify them of your non-resident status.
- Withdraw only for child-related expenses  
- Store receipts for compliance  
- Track dividends/capital gains for U.S. tax forms  

> **Pro Tip:** Keep a U.S. phone number (via a service like Google Voice or Tello) active to ensure you can receive 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication) codes for your bank and brokerage logins from your new resident country.

## At Child’s Majority Age (18–25)
- Child verifies identity online  
- Child takes ownership from abroad
- Child may:
  - Keep investing in U.S. markets  
  - Withdraw to their country of residence
  - Use funds for education or life goals
