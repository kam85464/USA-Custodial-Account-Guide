### Appendix: Foreign Tax Credit (FTC) Worksheet
When filing taxes in your resident country, use this logic to avoid paying tax twice:

1.  **Gross U.S. Income (A):** The total amount earned before taxes (found on your 1042-S/1099 form).
2.  **U.S. Tax Paid (B):** The amount withheld by the broker (usually 15% if W-8BEN is filed).
3.  **Local Tax Rate (C):** Your tax percentage in your resident country.
4.  **Local Tax Liability (D):** (A) x (C) = The tax your country wants to charge you.
5.  **Claimable Credit:** The **lower** of (B) or (D).

*Example:* If the U.S. took $15 in tax, and your local country wants $20, you pay the $20 but "subtract" the $15 already paid to the U.S. You only pay $5 locally.
